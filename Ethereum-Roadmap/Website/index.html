Launch
Dec 1, 2020

This marked the beginning of Ethereum's transition to a proof-of-stake consensus mechanism, and the genesis of what is now the consensus layer.

Altair
Oct 27, 2021

The first upgrade to the Beacon Chain. It introduced several improvements, but was mostly a trial run.

Bellatrix
Sep 6, 2022

A preparatory upgrade for the Beacon Chain that set the stage for the merge.

Merge
Sep 15, 2022

The transition from proof of work to proof of stake, triggered by reaching the Terminal Total Difficulty, and was not a hard fork.

Capella
Apr 12, 2023

Enabled validator withdrawals from the Beacon Chain, truly completing the transition to PoS.

Deneb
Soon

Upcoming upgrade that will help scale Layer 2s through blobs.

Client Teams

Software clients allow network participants to more easily interact with Ethereum, handling and obfuscating complex tasks and rules. For decentralization and redundancy sake, Ethereum opted for a multi-client approach. Every Ethereum upgrade must be coordinated and developed in tandem with the five teams in the appropriate layer(s).
