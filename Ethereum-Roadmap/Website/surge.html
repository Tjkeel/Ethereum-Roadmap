Proto-Danksharding

×

Status

Will go live in the next hard fork (Cancun-Deneb)

Summary

Proto-Danksharding introduces a new transaction type that carries large data "blobs," which are cheap to include on the consensus layer but are not processed by Ethereum's execution layer. This approach limits the computational burden on nodes, as they only store these blobs for a limited time. Proto-Danksharding lays the groundwork for future scalability upgrades, particularly full Danksharding, by setting up the necessary cryptographic and technical infrastructure.

Danksharding

×

Status

Ongoing research

Summary

To move beyond Proto-danksharding, and into Danksharding where we may attach more than 6 blobs to a given block, itâ€™s important that nodes only need to download a portion of the total blobs in a process referred to as data availability sampling. By doing so we can increase the number of blobs that may be attached to each block without increasing the load felt by a given node operator.

In addition to increasing the number of blobs that may be attached to a block, Danksharding may also increase the size of each of these blobs. Utilizing a process referred to as erasure coding, each node would not have to verify an entire blob, only a portion of it.

Ethereum's sharding roadmap has evolved from an idealistic, to a pragmatic rollup centric approach. As shown in the Ideation chart below, the Ethereum community original pursued sharding at the execution layer (Full Sharding). Gradually, and in conjunction with the rollup centric roadmap, leading research began to favor sharding only on the consensus layer (Data Sharding). Again, the community shifted in favor of Danksharding , which is not technically sharding at all, but rather a probabilistic data sampling approach requiring the introduction of second transaction type, blobs. Proto-Danksharding is the first iteration of this approach.

Ideation

Proto-Danksharding is slated for inclusion in the upcoming hard fork, Cancun-Deneb, as specified by EIP-4844. It is likely that Full Danksharding will follow piecemeal in a subsequent upgrade(s). In the medium term, it is reasonable to predict Danksharding will sufficiently scale rollups, and that additional protocol changes won't be necessary.
